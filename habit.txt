Part 1: The Meaning of a "Hint" (Cue)
​In the context of your notes, a "hint" (often called a cue or trigger) is the spark that starts an automatic behavior. It isn't just an object; it’s a psychological connection.
​Environmental Context: The most powerful hint is your environment. Since your physical surroundings (like your home) stay relatively consistent every day, they provide a stable foundation for habits.
​The Power of Sight: Among all senses, your eyes are the most influential body part for realizing and reacting to a hint.
​Relational Meaning: A hint is not just a physical "thing"; it is defined by your relationship with that thing. For example, one person sees a phone as a "hint" to play games, while another sees it as a "hint" to read.
​The Brain's "Code": Your brain creates a hardcoded rule: Hint A = Habit B. Once this connection is formed, it is nearly impossible to "delete" the link. You can stay away from the hint, but you cannot easily ban the brain from associating the hint with the habit.
​Part 2: Tips for Using Hints Effectively
​To change your behavior, you have to manipulate these "hints" intentionally. Here are the tips extracted from your notes:
​1. Build Awareness (The "Realize" Phase)
​The Habit Scorecard: Write down everything you do in a typical day. Mark them as "pros" or "cons" for both the short-term and long-term.
​Identity Shift: Use your identity to guide your habits. If you identify as a "healthy person," you use that self-image to reject hints that lead to overeating or staying up late.
​2. Implementation Intentions
​Define Time and Place: Don't be vague. Use the formula: "When I am in [A Place], hearing [B Thing], I will do [C Habit]." Clear positions make habits more automatic.
​3. Habit Stacking (The Loop)
​Link Hints Together: Use the completion of one habit as the "hint" for the next. For example, if you want to do homework, put it on your desk (the physical hint) so that the loop continues naturally.
​4. Optimize Your Environment
​Fresh Starts: It is easier to build a new habit in a new environment because there are no old "relationships" or "codes" tied to the space.
​Reduce Friction: If an environment is already tied to a bad habit (like overeating), it is very difficult to force a "good" habit (like exercising) in that same spot.
​Distance is Key: Since you can't "unlearn" a bad hint easily, the best strategy is to be far away from the hints that trigger bad habits.

​第一部分：提示（Hint）的意義
​在你的筆記中，「提示」不僅是一個觸發動作的訊號，更是大腦中的一種「心理連結」。
​環境即提示： 最強大的提示就是環境。因為你居住的空間每天相對固定，這種穩定性讓環境成為習慣最穩固的基底。
​視覺的主導性： 在所有感官中，眼睛是接收並意識到提示最核心的器官。
​提示是一種「關係」： 提示不只是物體本身，而是你與該物體的互動關係。例如：同樣的手機，對某些人是「玩遊戲」的提示，對另一部分人則是「閱讀」的提示。
​大腦的硬編碼： 你的大腦會建立一種規則：「提示 A = 習慣 B」。這是一種難以打破的心理連結。你雖然可以藉由「遠離提示」來避免惡習，但很難直接在大腦中「刪除」這個連結。
​第二部分：使用提示的技巧
​為了更有效地建立新習慣或戒除舊習慣，你可以參考以下技巧：
​1. 提升意識（「察覺」階段）
​習慣評分表： 寫下你典型的一天中做的所有事情。標註哪些是短期與長期來看的「優點（Pros）」或「缺點（Cons）」。
​身份認同： 用身份來定義自己。如果你認定自己是一個「健康的人」，你就會自動排斥熬夜或暴飲暴食的提示。
​2. 明確化執行意向
​定義時間與地點： 不要模糊其詞。使用這個公式：「當我在 [A 地點]、聽到 [B 聲音/事情] 時，我就做 [C 習慣]。」
​精確定位： 讓新習慣擁有明確的位置，這會讓行為更容易變得自動化。
​3. 建立習慣迴圈（Loop）
​疊加習慣： 讓前一個習慣的結束，成為下一個習慣的「提示」。例如：把功課放在書桌上，利用視覺提示來開啟學習的迴圈。
​UI 設計中的應用： 就像筆記中提到的，在 UI 介面上可以透過分隔線或無限節點的設計，讓使用者的動作形成一個循環。
​4. 優化環境
​新的環境，新的開始： 在新環境中建立新習慣最容易，因為那裡還沒有舊的「關係」或錯誤的連結。
​降低摩擦： 如果一個環境已經跟「暴飲暴食」高度連結，要在同一個地點強迫自己「運動」會非常困難。
​遠離誘惑： 既然大腦很難直接抹除提示，最強效的做法就是遠離那些會觸發惡習的提示。
